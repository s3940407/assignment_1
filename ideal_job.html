Brief description

The global company named ‘Accenture’ has a spot for the cyber incident response specialist position. Based on my understanding of the job description, this position is the first person that the representative of other departments (such as: human resources), go to when there is a cyber security related problem. This position also requires investigating on cases such as code of business ethics violation, malware and cyber-attacks such as hacking and security branches.

This position also requires employing others of technical, investigative and analytical skills in which are needed to form my own team in the department. I personally enjoy helping others who I think are worth helping such as employing individuals who are highly skilled and what I think are a great fit into the jobs required. I also have always dreamt of being a part of Accenture’s security team as they are one of the leading companies that has the capabilities in digital, cloud and security.

I am planning to continue two more years of studies after obtaining my bachelor’s degree for a Master of Cyber Security that is offered in RMIT. About CHFI

The company also require the candidate to be a holder of a couple computer forensics and information security certificates listed on the advertisement, one of which is the “Computer Hacking Forensic Investigator (CHFI)” certificate that is certified by the International Council of Electronic Commerce Consultants (EC-Council). A certified CHFI professional’s role is to detect a hacker attack whilst using multiple methods to extract evidence, reporting the crime and putting the system in place to prevent any future attacks. The CHFI program is targeted to IT professionals who are heavily involved with information system security, computer forensics and incident response.

About CISSP

Another certificate that is required for the job position is the “Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)” that is granted by the International Information System Security Certification Consortium (ISC2). Unlike the CHFI course, there are a few requirements to be met prior to be able to sit for the course and to obtain the certificate such as having a minimum of 5 years’ experience in full-time security field of at least two of the ISC2’s domains. Some of the domains include “security and risk management”, “communication and network security” and “security assessment and testing”. There is also a 3-hour multiple choice CISSP exam that must be taken and candidates must obtain at least 700 points out of the 1000 points available passing all eight domains of the test in order to be able to achieve the certificate.
